The History Of Tea In The World

Tea beverage is an infusion of dried leaves of Camellia sinensis, a member of Theaceae family. It is an evergreen shrub or tree that can grow to a height of 30ft, but is usually clipped to a height of 2.5ft in cultivation. Tea is the second- most consumed beverage after water. Freshly harvested tea leaves are processed differently in different parts of the world to give oolong tea (2%), green tea (20%) and black tea (78%)

Tea is nearly 5000 years old and was discovered, in 2737 BC by a renowned herbalist Shen Nung from China, when some leaves accidentally blew into a pot of boiling water. Tea is mainly grown in Asia and Africa, though also grown in South America and around the Black and Caspian Seas.

Tea leaves are usually plucked by hand with an average interval between plucking, is about a week. Usually, the terminal bud and two terminal leaves from the end of each shoot are plucked. In some cases, the bud and three leaves are taken giving a higher yield and poor-quality product.

Tea was first introduced to western priests and merchants in China, at which time it was termed cha. In India, tea industry originated after 1823 as result of discovery of tea plant in Assam.
